\title{Excavation-Induced Changes and Sensitivity of Near-Field Transport}
\author{Jan Březina, Jan Stebel, Pavel Exner, Petr Kabele, Tomáš Janda}
\date{May 2026}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
This work analyses excavation-induced changes in the rock mass and the sensitivity of contaminant transport through engineered barriers and the near field. The modelling uses data from experiments at the Bukov URF and focuses on interpreting pore-pressure measurements during excavation using hydro-mechanical (HM) models. A strong influence of fracture heterogeneity and foliation on pore-pressure time series is identified, and plausible physical interpretations of the observed signals are discussed. Comparison of linear-elastic and elasto-plastic excavation models indicates a damaged zone extent of approximately 0.5 m and confirms low error in predicting conductivity changes when using a linear-elastic HM approach. For inverse analysis, fracture heterogeneity must be included; its suitable parameterization remains open. The HM model is then used to predict excavation effects on permeability around access tunnels and deposition boreholes. A sensitivity analysis of transport to a nearby geological fault is performed using Sobol indices.
\end{abstract}
