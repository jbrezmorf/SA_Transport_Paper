\section{Model Parametrization}
\label{sec:model_parametrization}

This section compiles the subset of site, rock, and backfill information that
is directly needed by the transport study. It is extracted from the broader
review of \URFBII properties and from the bentonite chapter, but it is
intentionally limited to transport-relevant context.

\subsection{Site-Specific Data}
\label{sec:site_specific_data}

\subsubsection{Geological setting and role in the transport model}

The geological context of \URFBII is rooted in the complex geomechanics of the
12\textsuperscript{th} level of the \Rozna mine in the Czech Republic, situated
approximately 500--600 meters below the earth's surface. The research facility
consists of several galleries, each approximately 70--90 m long, accessed from
the main transport gallery P\v S1-123, see Fig.~\ref{fig:mapa}. The zone of
interest for the present study is around the L5 laboratory gallery and the
adjacent test chambers ZK5-1J and ZK5-1S.

\begin{figure}[htb]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{graphics/mapa_en.pdf}
    \caption{Situation plan of \URFBII.}
    \label{fig:mapa}
\end{figure}

The geology is heavily influenced by metamorphic processes, with structural
foliation affecting both mechanical and hydraulic properties. According to the
detailed geological model created by the Czech Geological Survey
(\cite{Bukovska2025final}), the lithologies present at the site primarily
include migmatitized amphibolite, migmatite, and biotite-amphibole paragneiss
migmatitized. For the transport study, this context is important mainly because
it supports the use of heterogeneous hydraulic properties and motivates
explicit representation of fracture-controlled pathways in the surrounding
rock.

\subsubsection{Hydraulic conductivity}
\label{sec:hydro_cond}

The review of laboratory and in-situ measurements indicates that the
intact-rock hydraulic conductivity spans several orders of magnitude, with
matrix-scale values typically near $10^{-13}$ to $10^{-11}\,\munit{m/s}$ and
substantially higher values observed in hydraulically active fracture zones.
For transport modelling, this supports two distinct roles of uncertainty:
relatively low matrix conductivity controlling diffusive retardation in the
rock mass, and fracture-related conductivity variability controlling
preferential flow and breakthrough.

\subsubsection{Porosity, storativity and Biot-Willis coefficient}
\label{sec:storativity}

Based on the available density and porosity data, the investigated rock massif
in \URFBII\ exhibits very low porosity, with total porosity mostly between 1
and 3~\% and open porosity generally below 1~\%. Such values are
characteristic of compact crystalline and metamorphic rocks and indicate
limited pore volume contributing to fluid storage. For modelling purposes, a
representative matrix porosity range
\[
\phi \approx 0.001\text{--}0.02
\]
can be adopted.

The Biot--Willis coefficient $\alpha$ is defined as
\[
\alpha = 1-\frac{K_D}{K_S},
\]
where $K_D$ is the drained bulk modulus of the rock and $K_S$ the bulk modulus
of the solid phase. In low-porosity, high-stiffness crystalline rocks, $K_D$
approaches $K_S$, leading to relatively small $\alpha$ values. Experimental
studies on dense crystalline and low-porosity rocks typically report
\[
\alpha \approx 0.1\text{--}0.3.
\]
Considering the measured porosity, the high stiffness of the metamorphic rocks
at the site of Rožná/Bukov, and published poromechanical data for similar
lithologies, this range is regarded as mechanically consistent for the intact
rock matrix. Using representative elastic parameters and water properties, the
resulting storativity is of the order
\[
S \approx 10^{-7}\text{--}10^{-5}\,\unit{\per\meter},
\]
which is typical for low-permeability crystalline rock environments.

\subsection{Transport Parameters of Saturated Pelletized Backfill}
\label{sec:transport_pellets}

This section provides transport parameters for radionuclide modelling in the
\emph{fully hydrated, long-term} state of the pelletized backfill (time scale
$\sim 10^3$~years). Transient resaturation and early-time preferential flow
during hydration are \emph{not} considered. The key design state variable is
the average emplaced dry density $\rho_d$; for the Czech concept we target
\[
\rho_d \approx 1.4~\mathrm{Mg/m^3}.
\]
Based on the FEBEX experiment results summarized in the bentonite support
material, we estimate an uncertainty range of $\pm 0.1~\mathrm{Mg/m^3}$,
corresponding to a standard deviation $\sigma_{\rho_d}=0.03~\mathrm{Mg/m^3}$.

\paragraph{Porosity and pore families (steady, saturated fabric).}
Using the measured solid density of BCV bentonite, $\rho_s=2.87~\mathrm{Mg/m^3}$,
the total porosity is
\[
n = 1-\frac{\rho_d}{\rho_s}\approx 0.51~\pm 0.04
\qquad \text{for}\qquad
\rho_d=1.40~\pm 0.1~\mathrm{Mg/m^3}.
\]

\paragraph{Effective diffusion.}
Following \cite{VanLoon2014_NTB1203}, we describe effective pore diffusion
$D_{e}$ in the saturated backfill by an extended Archie-type relation,
\[
D_{e} = D_{w}\,\varepsilon^{m_1} + B\,\varepsilon^{m_2},
\]
where $D_w$ is the diffusivity in free water and $(m_1,m_2,B)$ are empirical
parameters taken from the compacted-bentonite parameterisation in Tab.~28 of
\cite{VanLoon2014_NTB1203}. For neutral tracers and most cations, we take
$\varepsilon_i \approx n$ (total porosity). For anions, anion exclusion reduces
the diffusion-accessible porosity. Using the porosity range stated above and
the parameter envelope from \cite{VanLoon2014_NTB1203}, we retain the following
representative log-diffusivity values at $25^\circ$C:
\begin{align*}
Y_{\mathrm{neu}} &= -9.4,& \sigma_{\mathrm{neu}}&=0.1,\\
Y_{\mathrm{cat}} &= -9.8,& \sigma_{\mathrm{cat}}&=0.1,\\
Y_{\mathrm{an}} &= -10.9,& \sigma_{\mathrm{an}}&=0.3,\\
Y_{\mathrm{act}} &= -13.1,& \sigma_{\mathrm{act}}&=0.2.
\end{align*}

\paragraph{Advection and dispersion in the long-term saturated state.}
Following first-order stochastic theory for macroscopic dispersion
(\cite{Zech_Evidence_2023}), we express the longitudinal macrodispersivity as
\begin{equation}
    \label{eq:dispersivity}
    \alpha_L = \sigma_{\ln K}^2\, I_h,
\end{equation}
where $\sigma_{\ln K}^2=\Var(\ln K)$ is the variance of the log-conductivity
field and $I_h$ is the longitudinal integral scale. The integral scale is
expected to be in the range
\[
I_h \approx 0.1\text{--}1~\mathrm{m},
\]
associated with hydration-related inhomogeneities. Using the fitted relation
between dry density and conductivity, we obtain
\[
\sigma_{\log_{10}K} = 6 \sigma_{\rho_d} + 0.08 = 0.26,
\]
corresponding to $\sigma^2_{\ln K}\approx 0.36$, and therefore
\[
\alpha_L \approx 0.026\text{--}0.26~\mathrm{m}.
\]

These backfill parameters are extracted here because they directly enter the
transport uncertainty model, while the broader bentonite discussion remains in
the support material.
